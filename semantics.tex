\section{Formal Model}
\label{sec:semantics}

This section gives an operational semantics for \hadl that alternates evaluation, type checking, and policy checking, with self-healing when any check fails. The central challenge is stating what \emph{correctness} means for a language whose types, workflows, and authorization policies materialize at runtime and whose reduction deliberately visits ill-typed intermediate states during self-heal. We address this by (i) parameterizing over a non-deterministic oracle, (ii) tracking a runtime-typed environment that grows during reduction, and (iii) stating soundness relative to an oracle that is eventually-truthful within the declared retry budget.

\subsection{Machine Configuration}
\label{sec:sem-config}

A \hadl configuration is a five-tuple
\[
  C \;=\; \MConfig{\MEnv}{\MErr}{\MPol}{\MPrin}{e}
\]
whose components are summarized in \figr{fig:config}.

\begin{figure}[t]
\centering
\begin{tabular}{@{}ll@{}}
\toprule
Component & Meaning \\
\midrule
$\MEnv : x \mapsto (v,\tau,p,m)$ & Typed environment binding each variable to its value $v$, runtime \\
  & type $\tau$, provenance $p$, and mutability flag $m \in \{\texttt{let},\texttt{var}\}$. \\
$\MErr$ & Heal context: list of feedback strings $[\epsilon_1,\ldots,\epsilon_k]$ from \\
  & prior failed oracle attempts. Flushed on successful binding. \\
$\MPol$ & Installed Cedar policy. Updated by $\Enforce{x}$ via append-only \\
  & merge; only tightens. \\
$\MPrin$ & Current principal, inherited from the surrounding $\texttt{as}$ clause \\
  & or defaulted to \texttt{"gen"} / \texttt{"agent"}. \\

\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
\caption{Components of a \hadl machine configuration.}
\label{fig:config}
\end{figure}

Values $v$ include base literals, records, arrays, workflow ASTs (for arrow-typed binders), Cedar policy values, and \emph{schema values} $\Tschema(\tau)$ that reify a concrete type at runtime. The order in which sub-expressions reduce is fixed by a syntactic extraction function $\mathtt{Extract}$ (\S\ref{sec:sem-reduction}), which surfaces the leftmost oracle-head (a $\Gen{\tau}{s}$ or $\Agent{s}$) for the step relation to act on.

\subsection{Judgments}
\label{sec:sem-judgments}

The formal model uses four judgments that interlock runtime values, static types, authorization, and configuration well-formedness.

\paragraph{Runtime typing} $\RtType{\MEnv}{v}{\tau}$ holds when value $v$ has type $\tau$ under environment $\MEnv$. When $\tau$ is a variable name bound in $\MEnv$ to a schema value $\Tschema(\tau')$, the judgment resolves through $\MEnv$ and checks $\RtType{\MEnv}{v}{\tau'}$. Record and array cases recurse structurally.

\paragraph{Residual static typing} $\StType{\Projenv{\MEnv}}{e}{\tau}$ holds when expression $e$ has type $\tau$ under the \emph{projected} static context $\Projenv{\MEnv} = \{x{:}\tau \mid \MEnv(x) = (\_,\tau,\_,\_)\}$. This is the ordinary static type system of \hadl applied to the residual program after reduction has extended the environment. It is invoked at compile time on the initial program and re-invoked at runtime after every materialization.\footnote{The mechanization treats $\StType{\cdot}{\cdot}{\cdot}$ as a black-box oracle (only its reflexivity on reduced values and on $\Tschema$-annotated expressions is used). Because $\rulename{Gen-Success}$ re-invokes it as a side condition, (T1) is unaffected.}

\paragraph{Policy authorization} $\PolOk{\MPol}{\MPrin}{a}$ holds when Cedar policy $\MPol$ permits action $a \in \{\texttt{gen},\texttt{agent},\texttt{eval}\}$ for principal $\MPrin$ against the current resource. It is the Cedar decision procedure lifted into our judgment language.

\paragraph{Configuration well-formedness} $\WF{C}$ holds when:
\begin{enumerate}
  \item every binding $\MBind{x}{v}{\tau}{p}{m}$ in $\MEnv$ satisfies $\RtType{\MEnv}{v}{\tau}$;
  \item the residual $e$ type-checks: $\StType{\Projenv{\MEnv}}{e}{\tau}$ for some $\tau$;
  \item the provenance relation induced by $\MEnv$ is acyclic; and
  \item $|\MErr| \le \MRetry$.
\end{enumerate}

\subsection{Reduction}
\label{sec:sem-reduction}

The reduction relation $C \step C'$ is the top-level closure, over a partial extraction function $\mathtt{Extract}$, of a pure-core small-step relation $\stepP$. $\Extr{e}$ returns either $(pre,\,x,\,suf)$ --- where $pre$ is the leftmost oracle head in $e$ (a $\Gen{\tau}{s}$ or $\Agent{s}$), $x$ is a fresh binder drawn from a reserved namespace disjoint from source identifiers, and $suf$ is the residual obtained by replacing that occurrence of $pre$ with $x$ --- or $\bot$ when no oracle head is visible. \figr{fig:extract} gives its defining clauses. Reduction then decomposes into two layers glued by $\mathtt{Extract}$: a pure core $\stepP$ (L1), closed under congruence for sequencing, conditionals, loops, and binders; and an oracle-execution step (L2) that fires on extracted heads. Policy installation (L3) lives in L1 and propagates through congruence.

    &= (p,\;y,\;\Seq{\Let{x}{\tau}{e_1'}}{e_2}) \; \text{if } \Extr{e_1}=(p,y,e_1') \\

\paragraph{L1: pure core.} $\rulename{Var}$, $\rulename{Let-Bind}$, $\rulename{Assign}$, $\rulename{If-True}$/$\rulename{If-False}$, $\rulename{While}$, $\rulename{For}$, $\rulename{Js}$, $\rulename{Seq}$, and $\rulename{Enforce-Install}$ form a standard call-by-value small-step relation $\stepP$ closed under congruence (exemplified in Fig.~\ref{fig:rules-core} by $\rulename{Let-Cong}$; the remaining congruence rules live in Appendix~\ref{app:rules}). They preserve $\MErr$, $\MPol$, and $\MPrin$ except that $\rulename{Let-Bind}$ flushes $\MErr$ to $\emptyset$ and $\rulename{Enforce-Install}$ updates $\MPol$.

\paragraph{L2: gen layer.} When $\Extr{e} = (\Gen{\tau}{s},\,x,\,\mathit{suf})$, the extracted gen is run at the top level through four side conditions: a policy pre-check, an oracle call, a runtime-type check, and a residual re-type-check against $\mathit{suf}$ under the extended environment. $\rulename{Gen-Success}$ fires only when all four succeed; the residual $\mathit{suf}$ (with $x$ bound in $\MEnv'$) replaces $e$. When the oracle returns a value that fails the runtime-type check, $\rulename{Gen-Heal-Type}$ appends a diagnostic to $\MErr$ and keeps $e$; the next step will re-extract the same $\Gen{\tau}{s}$, this time with the enlarged heal context visible to the oracle. $\rulename{Gen-Heal-Pol}$ handles the symmetric policy-rejection case. $\rulename{Gen-Budget-Exhausted}$ fires deterministically when the heal context has reached $\MRetry$ and the most recent attempt still fails; it transitions to a terminal error configuration recording both $\MErr$ and the provenance label of the causal $\Gen{\tau}{s}$ call.

\paragraph{L3: policy layer.} $\rulename{Enforce-Install}$ is a pure-core rule that propagates to any position through congruence: at any sub-expression position for which $\Extr{e} = \bot$, $\rulename{GStep-Pure}$ lifts $\rulename{Enforce-Install}$ to a top-level step. The install operation (i) rejects any blanket $\texttt{forbid}$ policy under the deny-all guard, (ii) rejects unknown action types, (iii) merges the rules append-only, and (iv) checks that every $\texttt{as}$ clause reachable before the next $\Enforce$ names a principal defined in the merged policy. The side condition $\Allow{\MPol'} \subseteq \Allow{\MPol}$ makes monotone tightening an integral part of the rule: any install that widens permissions cannot fire. $\rulename{Enforce-Heal}$ (in the appendix) mirrors $\rulename{Gen-Heal-Type}$ for the case where the underlying policy value fails to compile.

\subsection{Correctness}
\label{sec:sem-correctness}

Classical progress and preservation do not apply directly because \hadl types are runtime values, the residual type context grows during reduction, policies evolve, and self-heal steps deliberately visit ill-typed configurations. We state a single combined theorem that captures what \emph{is} preserved, parameterized by the oracle.

\begin{definition}[Eventually-truthful oracle]
\label{def:oracle-truthful}
An oracle $\mathcal{O}$ is \emph{eventually-truthful at budget $\MRetry$} if, for every gen site $\Gen{\tau}{s}$, every environment $\MEnv$, every policy $\MPol$, and every principal $\MPrin$ such that $\PolOk{\MPol}{\MPrin}{\texttt{gen}}$, there exists a heal context $\epsilon$ with $|\epsilon| \le \MRetry$ and a value $v$ satisfying both $\Oracle{s}{\epsilon}{\tau} \ni v$ and $\RtType{\MEnv}{v}{\tau}$.
\end{definition}

This point-wise phrasing matches the form used in the mechanization: it quantifies once over each \emph{call site}, not over traces. The trace-level reading (at each gen encountered, some $k\le N$ oracle response discharges the side conditions) follows by instantiating $(\MEnv,\MPol,\MPrin)$ with the configuration reached when that gen is about to fire.

\begin{theorem}[\hadl soundness]
\label{thm:hadl-sound}
Let $\mathcal{O}$ be any oracle and let $C_0 = \MConfig{\emptyset}{\emptyset}{\MPol_0}{\MPrin_0}{e_0}$ be an initial configuration with $\WF{C_0}$. Then every reduction trace $C_0 \step C_1 \step \cdots$ satisfies:\footnote{Clauses (T1)--(T3), (T4a), and (T4b) are mechanized in Lean~4 under \texttt{mechanization/} in the artifact, sorry-free, covering every rule in \S\ref{sec:semantics} and Appendix~\ref{app:rules}. Trace-level progress for the gen case of (T4c) is additionally proved under a source-program hygiene hypothesis that reserves the $\mathtt{Extract}$ binder namespace (Appendix~\ref{app:extract}); the agent case is analogous. The pure-core termination measure underlying (T4c) is argued only on paper.}
\begin{enumerate}
  \item \textbf{\textsc{(T1) WF-Preservation.}} For every $n$ with $C_n$ not a terminal error, $\WF{C_n}$.
  \item \textbf{\textsc{(T2) Staged Materialization Soundness.}} Whenever $C_n \step C_{n+1}$ is an application of $\rulename{Gen-Success}$ with $\Extr{e_n} = (\Gen{\tau}{s},\,x,\,\mathit{suf})$, the expression $e_{n+1}$ of $C_{n+1}$ --- namely $\mathit{suf}$ with $x$ now bound to the materialized $v$ in $\MEnv_{n+1}$ --- type-checks against $\Projenv{\MEnv_{n+1}}$; every subsequent $\Gen{\tau'}{s'}$ with an annotation referring to a name newly bound at step $n{+}1$ is checked against the materialized concrete type.
  \item \textbf{\textsc{(T3) Policy Monotonicity.}} For $m \le n$, $\Allow{\MPol_n} \subseteq \Allow{\MPol_m}$; and every $\rulename{Gen-Success}$, $\rulename{Agent-Success}$, or $\rulename{Eval-Success}$ step between $m$ and $n$ satisfied the policy-authorization side condition under $\MPol_k$ and $\MPrin_k$ at its point of application.
  \item \textbf{\textsc{(T4a) Budget Progress.}} If at some $C_n$ we have $\Extr{e_n} = (\Gen{\tau}{s},\,x,\,\mathit{suf})$ and $|\MErr_n| > \MRetry$, then $\rulename{Gen-Budget-Exhausted}$ fires at step $n$ and yields a terminal error configuration carrying $\MErr_n$ and the gen's provenance label.
  \item \textbf{\textsc{(T4b) Truthful Gen-Success.}} If $\mathcal{O}$ is eventually-truthful at budget $\MRetry$ (\defn{def:oracle-truthful}) and at $C_n$ we have $\Extr{e_n} = (\Gen{\tau}{s},\,x,\,\mathit{suf})$ with $\PolOk{\MPol_n}{\MPrin_n}{\texttt{gen}}$, then there exist $\epsilon$ with $|\epsilon|\le\MRetry$ and $v$ with $\RtType{\MEnv_n}{v}{\tau}$ such that $\rulename{Gen-Success}$ fires from $C_n[\MErr_n := \epsilon]$ and flushes the heal context to $\emptyset$.
  \item \textbf{\textsc{(T4c) Oracle-Relative Termination.}} If $\mathcal{O}$ is eventually-truthful at budget $\MRetry$ and all pure loops in $e_0$ terminate, then $C_0 \steps C_\text{final}$ with $e_\text{final}$ a value. If at some gen site $\mathcal{O}$ is not eventually-truthful, the trace terminates in a terminal error configuration $\mathsf{err}(\MErr,\Prov(\Gen{\tau}{s}))$ whose provenance label identifies the specific $\Gen{\tau}{s}$ call that exhausted its budget.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}[Proof sketch]
By induction on the length of the reduction trace. (T1), (T3), (T4a), (T4b) are proved per-rule, mirroring the Lean mechanization; (T2) is a direct rereading of the $\rulename{Gen-Success}$ side condition; (T4c) combines eventually-truthful gen-success (T4b) with a well-founded measure on intermediate heal steps. Full proofs appear in Appendix~\ref{app:proofs}.
\end{proof}

The following corollaries specialize the theorem to two properties often stated independently in the literature.

\begin{corollary}[Bounded-retry termination]
\label{cor:bounded-retry}
Assume (i) $\mathcal{O}$ is eventually-truthful at budget $\MRetry$ at every gen site encountered in the trace, and (ii) all pure loops in $e_0$ terminate. Then every reduction trace from a well-formed $C_0$ reaches either a value configuration or a terminal error configuration in finitely many steps. Without (i), the trace may still terminate in a terminal error by (T4a); without (ii), termination for $\rulename{Js}$-dominated fragments is delegated to the JavaScript host and not guaranteed by \hadl alone.
\end{corollary}

\begin{corollary}[Blame faithfulness]
\label{cor:blame}
Assume the source program $e_0$ contains no occurrence of the internal $\mathsf{errTerm}(\cdot,\cdot)$ construct (i.e., only surface syntax of \figr{fig:syntax}). If a trace ends in a terminal error configuration $\mathsf{err}(\MErr,\ell)$, then $\ell$ is the source label of the specific $\Gen{\tau}{s}$ call whose budget was exhausted, and $\MErr$ records the sequence of diagnostic feedback for that call.
\end{corollary}
