\section{Formal Model}
\label{sec:semantics}

This section gives an operational semantics for \hadl that alternates evaluation, type checking, and policy checking, with self-healing when any check fails. The central challenge is stating what \emph{correctness} means for a language whose types, workflows, and authorization policies materialize at runtime and whose reduction deliberately visits ill-typed intermediate states during self-heal. We address this by (i) parameterizing over a non-deterministic oracle, (ii) tracking a runtime-typed environment that grows during reduction, and (iii) stating soundness relative to an oracle that is eventually-truthful within the declared retry budget.

\subsection{Machine Configuration}
\label{sec:sem-config}

A \hadl configuration is a five-tuple
\[
  C \;=\; \MConfig{\MEnv}{\MErr}{\MPol}{\MPrin}{e}
\]
whose components are summarized in \figr{fig:config}.

\begin{figure}[t]
\centering
\begin{tabular}{@{}ll@{}}
\toprule
Component & Meaning \\
\midrule
$\MEnv : x \mapsto (v,\tau,m)$ & Typed environment binding each variable to its value $v$, runtime \\
  & type $\tau$, and mutability flag $m \in \{\texttt{let},\texttt{var}\}$. \\
$\MErr$ & Heal context: append-only log of events, each either $\ErrEv{\epsilon}$ \\
  & carrying feedback from a failed oracle attempt, or $\SucEv$ marking \\
  & a successful materialization. The retry metric $\retries{\MErr}$ is \\
  & the length of the trailing run of $\ErrEv{\cdot}$ events. \\
$\MPol$ & Installed Cedar policy. Updated by $\Enforce{x}$ via append-only \\
  & merge; only tightens. \\
$\MPrin$ & Current principal, inherited from the surrounding $\texttt{as}$ clause \\

\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
\caption{Components of a \hadl machine configuration.}

Values $v$ include base literals, records, arrays, workflow ASTs (for arrow-typed binders), Cedar policy values, and \emph{schema values} $\Tschema(\tau)$ that reify a concrete type at runtime. The order in which sub-expressions reduce is the standard left-to-right call-by-value (CBV) discipline: explicit structural congruence rules (\S\ref{sec:sem-reduction}) push reduction to the leftmost sub-term at each compound construct, so an oracle head ($\Gen{\tau}{s}$ or $\Agent{s}$) fires once it surfaces as the leftmost redex.

\subsection{Judgments}
\label{sec:sem-judgments}

The formal model uses four judgments that interlock runtime values, static types, authorization, and configuration well-formedness.

\paragraph{Runtime typing} $\RtType{\MEnv}{v}{\tau}$ holds when value $v$ has type $\tau$ under environment $\MEnv$. When $\tau$ is a variable name bound in $\MEnv$ to a schema value $\Tschema(\tau')$, the judgment resolves through $\MEnv$ and checks $\RtType{\MEnv}{v}{\tau'}$. Record and array cases recurse structurally.

\paragraph{Residual static typing} $\StType{\Projenv{\MEnv}}{e}{\tau}$ holds when expression $e$ has type $\tau$ under the \emph{projected} static context $\Projenv{\MEnv} = \{x{:}\tau \mid \MEnv(x) = (\_,\tau,\_)\}$. This is the ordinary static type system of \hadl applied to the residual program after reduction has extended the environment. It is invoked at compile time on the initial program and re-invoked at runtime after every materialization.\footnote{The mechanization treats $\StType{\cdot}{\cdot}{\cdot}$ as a black-box oracle (only its reflexivity on reduced values and on $\Tschema$-annotated expressions is used). Because $\rulename{Gen-Success}$ re-invokes it as a side condition, (T1) is unaffected.}

\paragraph{Policy authorization} $\PolOk{\MPol}{\MPrin}{a}$ holds when Cedar policy $\MPol$ permits action $a \in \{\texttt{gen},\texttt{agent},\texttt{eval}\}$ for principal $\MPrin$ against the current resource. It is the Cedar decision procedure lifted into our judgment language.

\paragraph{Configuration well-formedness} $\WF{C}$ holds when:
\begin{enumerate}
  \item every binding $\MBind{x}{v}{\tau}{m}$ in $\MEnv$ satisfies $\RtType{\MEnv}{v}{\tau}$;
  \item the residual $e$ type-checks: $\StType{\Projenv{\MEnv}}{e}{\tau}$ for some $\tau$; and
  \item $\retries{\MErr} \le \MRetry$.
\end{enumerate}

\subsection{Reduction}
\label{sec:sem-reduction}

The reduction relation $C \step C'$ is a single small-step relation with two kinds of rules: \emph{root-level} rules that fire when the top of $e$ matches the rule head exactly (variables, let-binding, if-true/false, oracle heads $\Gen{\tau}{s}$ and $\Agent{s}$, policy install, \ldots), and \emph{structural congruence} rules that drive reduction to the leftmost sub-term at each compound construct --- one per binder (let, assign, if, seq, for). The congruence rules impose the standard CBV left-to-right discipline: $\rulename{Let-Cong}$ reduces $e_1$ under $\Let{x}{\tau}{e_1}{e_2}$ until $e_1$ is a value, at which point $\rulename{Let-Bind}$ fires at the root; analogous rules handle the other constructs. Policy installation (L3) is itself a root rule and therefore propagates through congruence automatically.

\paragraph{L1: pure core.} $\rulename{Var}$, $\rulename{Let-Bind}$, $\rulename{Assign}$, $\rulename{If-True}$/$\rulename{If-False}$, $\rulename{While}$, $\rulename{For}$, $\rulename{Js}$, $\rulename{Seq}$, and $\rulename{Enforce-Install}$ form a standard call-by-value small-step relation. Each is a \emph{root} rule: it fires only when the expression matches the rule head at the top. Reduction drives to the leftmost sub-term via the structural congruence rules (exemplified in Fig.~\ref{fig:rules-core} by $\rulename{Let-Cong}$; the remaining congruence rules live in Appendix~\ref{app:rules}). Each congruence rule carries a $\neq \mathsf{err}$ side condition on the inner step so a terminal error does not re-enter evaluation. Root rules preserve $\MErr$ and $\MPrin$ except that $\rulename{Enforce-Install}$ updates $\MPol$. $\MErr$ is append-only throughout reduction; it is never flushed.

\paragraph{L2: gen layer.} When the leftmost redex is $\Gen{\tau}{s}$, the gen rules fire directly at the root without any extraction or rebinding: $\rulename{Gen-Success}$ runs the gen through three side conditions --- a policy pre-check, an oracle call, and a runtime-type check --- and on success replaces the head with the value $v$ itself, appending a $\SucEv$ to $\MErr$. The surrounding context (e.g.~the body of a $\rulename{Let-Cong}$) threads $v$ downstream through normal CBV substitution via $\rulename{Let-Bind}$ once $v$ is ready; the residual is re-type-checked implicitly by that subsequent $\rulename{Let-Bind}$ step, not as an extra side condition on $\rulename{Gen-Success}$ itself. When the oracle returns a value that fails the runtime-type check, $\rulename{Gen-Heal-Type}$ appends an $\ErrEv{\cdot}$ diagnostic to $\MErr$ and keeps the $\Gen{\tau}{s}$ head in place; the next step retries the same head with the enlarged feedback log visible to the oracle. $\rulename{Gen-Heal-Pol}$ handles the symmetric policy-rejection case. $\rulename{Gen-Budget-Exhausted}$ fires deterministically when the trailing $\ErrEv{\cdot}$ run in $\MErr$ exceeds $\MRetry$; it transitions to a terminal error configuration recording both $\MErr$ and the source-span projection $\Prov(\Gen{\tau}{s})$ of the causal call.

\paragraph{L3: policy layer.} $\rulename{Enforce-Install}$ is a root rule that propagates to any position through the structural congruence rules. The install operation (i) rejects any blanket $\texttt{forbid}$ policy under the deny-all guard, (ii) rejects unknown action types, (iii) merges the rules append-only, and (iv) checks that every $\texttt{as}$ clause reachable before the next $\Enforce$ names a principal defined in the merged policy. The side condition $\Allow{\MPol'} \subseteq \Allow{\MPol}$ makes monotone tightening an integral part of the rule: any install that widens permissions cannot fire. $\rulename{Enforce-Heal}$ (in the appendix) mirrors $\rulename{Gen-Heal-Type}$ for the case where the underlying policy value fails to compile.

\subsection{Correctness}
\label{sec:sem-correctness}

Classical progress and preservation do not apply directly because \hadl types are runtime values, the residual type context grows during reduction, policies evolve, and self-heal steps deliberately visit ill-typed configurations. We decompose what \emph{is} preserved into three theorems, all parameterized by the oracle: \emph{Soundness} states the per-step invariants (well-formedness, materialization correctness, policy shrinkage); \emph{Progress} states that an oracle head can always take a step (either a successful materialization or a terminal-error marker); \emph{Termination} lifts these to whole traces.

\begin{definition}[Eventually-truthful oracle]
\label{def:oracle-truthful}
An oracle $\mathcal{O}$ is \emph{eventually-truthful at budget $\MRetry$} if, for every gen site $\Gen{\tau}{s}$, every environment $\MEnv$, every policy $\MPol$, and every principal $\MPrin$ such that $\PolOk{\MPol}{\MPrin}{\texttt{gen}}$, there exists a heal context $\MErr$ with $\retries{\MErr} \le \MRetry$ and a value $v$ satisfying both $\Oracle{s}{\MErr}{\tau} \ni v$ and $\RtType{\MEnv}{v}{\tau}$.
\end{definition}

This point-wise phrasing matches the form used in the mechanization: it quantifies once over each \emph{call site}, not over traces. The trace-level reading (at each gen encountered, some $k\le\MRetry$ oracle response discharges the side conditions) follows by instantiating $(\MEnv,\MPol,\MPrin)$ with the configuration reached when that gen is about to fire.

\begin{theorem}[Soundness]
\label{thm:hadl-soundness}
Let $\mathcal{O}$ be any oracle and let $C_0 = \MConfig{\emptyset}{\emptyset}{\MPol_0}{\MPrin_0}{e_0}$ be an initial configuration with $\WF{C_0}$. Then along every reduction trace $C_0 \step C_1 \step \cdots$:
\begin{enumerate}
  \item[(a)] \emph{Well-formedness preservation.} $\WF{C_n}$ holds for every $n$ with $C_n$ not a terminal error.
  \item[(b)] \emph{Materialization correctness.} Whenever $C_n \step C_{n+1}$ applies $\rulename{Gen-Success}$ at a gen head $\Gen{\tau}{s}$ (resp.\ $\rulename{Agent-Success}$, $\rulename{Eval-Success}$), the produced value $v$ satisfies $\RtType{\MEnv_n}{v}{\tau}$. When the head occurs under a binder such as $\Let{x}{\tau}{\Gen{\tau}{s}}{e'}$, a subsequent $\rulename{Let-Bind}$ step extends $\MEnv$ with $\MBind{x}{v}{\tau}{\texttt{let}}$, and every subsequent $\Gen{\tau'}{s'}$ whose annotation refers to $x$ is checked against the materialized concrete type rather than against $\Tschema$.
  \item[(c)] \emph{Policy monotonicity.} For $m \le n$, $\Allow{\MPol_n}\subseteq\Allow{\MPol_m}$; and every $\rulename{Gen-Success}$, $\rulename{Agent-Success}$, or $\rulename{Eval-Success}$ step between $m$ and $n$ satisfied the policy-authorization side condition under $\MPol_k$ and $\MPrin_k$ at its point of application.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}

\begin{theorem}[Progress]
\label{thm:hadl-progress}
Fix $C_n$ with $\WF{C_n}$ whose leftmost redex is an oracle head $\Gen{\tau}{s}$ (resp.\ $\Agent{s}$).
\begin{enumerate}
  \item[(a)] \emph{Budget exhaustion.} If $\retries{\MErr_n}>\MRetry$, then $\rulename{Gen-Budget-Exhausted}$ (resp.\ the agent analogue) fires, lifted by the structural congruence rules to the redex position, yielding a terminal error configuration $\mathsf{err}(\MErr_n,\Prov(\Gen{\tau}{s}))$.
  \item[(b)] \emph{Truthful success.} If $\mathcal{O}$ is eventually-truthful at budget $\MRetry$ (\defn{def:oracle-truthful}) and $\PolOk{\MPol_n}{\MPrin_n}{\texttt{gen}}$ (resp.\ $\texttt{agent}$), there exist $\MErr'$ with $\retries{\MErr'}\le\MRetry$ and a value $v$ with $\RtType{\MEnv_n}{v}{\tau}$ such that $\rulename{Gen-Success}$ (resp.\ $\rulename{Agent-Success}$) fires from $C_n[\MErr_n := \MErr']$, appending $\SucEv$ and resetting the retry metric to~$0$.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}

\begin{theorem}[Termination]
\label{thm:hadl-term}
Let $C_0$ be a well-formed initial configuration. Assume (i) every \texttt{while}/\texttt{for} loop in $e_0$ terminates, and (ii) $e_0$ contains no occurrence of the internal $\mathsf{errTerm}(\cdot,\cdot)$ construct. Then every trace from $C_0$ reaches in finitely many steps either a value configuration or a terminal-error configuration $\mathsf{err}(\MErr,\Prov(\Gen{\tau}{s}))$. In the second case, $\Prov(\Gen{\tau}{s})$ is the source-span projection of the specific gen call whose retry budget was exhausted, and $\MErr$ records the sequence of $\ErrEv{\cdot}$ feedback events leading up to that failure (interleaved with $\SucEv$ markers from any earlier successful gens). If, in addition, $\mathcal{O}$ is eventually-truthful at budget $\MRetry$ at every gen site encountered along the trace, then the trace terminates in a value configuration.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}[Proof sketch]
Soundness is proved by induction on the $\mathtt{Step}$ constructor, per-rule, mirroring the Lean mechanization: (a) follows from the WF-preservation lemma, (b) is a direct read-off of the $\rulename{*-Success}$ rules' runtime-type premises, (c) follows from the allow-set shrinkage baked into $\rulename{Enforce-Install}$ together with the authorization premises on the success rules. Progress is a case split on $\retries{\MErr_n}>\MRetry$ vs.\ $\le\MRetry$: the former invokes $\rulename{Gen-Budget-Exhausted}$ directly, the latter invokes eventually-truthfulness and discharges the $\rulename{Gen-Success}$ premises. Termination combines Progress with a well-founded measure on intermediate heal steps --- at most $\MRetry$ heal steps can fire between two successive materialization successes, and the number of distinct oracle sites reached along the trace is finite. Full proofs appear in Appendix~\ref{app:proofs}.
\end{proof}

\noindent\textbf{Mechanization.}
Theorem~\ref{thm:hadl-soundness} and Theorem~\ref{thm:hadl-progress} are mechanized in Lean~4 under \texttt{mechanization/} in the artifact, sorry-free and depending only on Lean's standard meta-axioms ($\mathtt{propext}$, $\mathtt{Classical.choice}$, $\mathtt{Quot.sound}$).\footnote{The paper's $\step$ is formalized as a single inductive relation $\mathtt{Step}$ (\texttt{mechanization/HADL/Reduction.lean}) containing the root rules of~\figr{fig:rules-core} together with five CBV structural-congruence rules (one per binder).  Theorem~\ref{thm:hadl-soundness} decomposes into the Lean theorems $\mathtt{T1\_WF\_preservation}$ (a), $\mathtt{T1\_materialize\_RtType}$ (b), and $\mathtt{T3\_policy\_monotonicity}$ (c).  Theorem~\ref{thm:hadl-progress} decomposes into $\mathtt{T4\_truthful\_success}$ / $\mathtt{T4\_truthful\_success\_agent}$ (b) lifted to all CBV positions by $\mathtt{T4\_progress\_gen}$ / $\mathtt{T4\_progress\_agent}$; (a) is an immediate consequence of the $\mathtt{Step.genBudgetExhausted}$ constructor being applicable whenever its $\retries{\cdot}>\MRetry$ premise holds, also lifted by the congruence rules.}  The trace-level well-founded measure underlying Theorem~\ref{thm:hadl-term} is argued on paper only: all \emph{per-step} ingredients are mechanized, but the overall trace termination invokes a finiteness assumption on the number of oracle sites that is not formalized.
