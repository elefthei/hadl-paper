\section{Full Reduction Rules}
\label{app:rules}

This appendix gives the reduction rules elided from \sect{sec:sem-reduction}. Evaluation contexts $E$ are the standard left-to-right contexts over sequencing, conditionals, loops, assignment right-hand sides, and primitive arguments.

\subsection{Pure Core (L1)}
\label{app:rules-core}

\begin{mathpar}
\inferrule*[right=\rulename{Assign}]
  {\MEnv(x) = (\_,\tau,\texttt{var})}
  {\MConfig{\MEnv}{\MErr}{\MPol}{\MPrin}{\Assign{x}{v}} \step \MConfig{\MEnv[\MBind{x}{v}{\tau}{\texttt{var}}]}{\MErr}{\MPol}{\MPrin}{\Tunit}}

\inferrule*[right=\rulename{Var-Bind}]
  { }
  {\MConfig{\MEnv}{\MErr}{\MPol}{\MPrin}{\Var{x}{\tau}{v}} \step \MConfig{\MEnv[\MBind{x}{v}{\tau}{\texttt{var}}]}{\MErr}{\MPol}{\MPrin}{\Tunit}}

\inferrule*[right=\rulename{If-True}]
  { }
  {\MConfig{\MEnv}{\MErr}{\MPol}{\MPrin}{\Iff{\texttt{true}}{e_1}{e_2}} \step \MConfig{\MEnv}{\MErr}{\MPol}{\MPrin}{e_1}}

\inferrule*[right=\rulename{If-False}]
  { }
  {\MConfig{\MEnv}{\MErr}{\MPol}{\MPrin}{\Iff{\texttt{false}}{e_1}{e_2}} \step \MConfig{\MEnv}{\MErr}{\MPol}{\MPrin}{e_2}}

\inferrule*[right=\rulename{While}]
  { }
  {\MConfig{\MEnv}{\MErr}{\MPol}{\MPrin}{\Whl{e}{e'}} \step \MConfig{\MEnv}{\MErr}{\MPol}{\MPrin}{\Iff{e}{\Seq{e'}{\Whl{e}{e'}}}{\Tunit}}}

\inferrule*[right=\rulename{For-Nil}]
  { }
  {\MConfig{\MEnv}{\MErr}{\MPol}{\MPrin}{\Forr{x}{[]}{e}} \step \MConfig{\MEnv}{\MErr}{\MPol}{\MPrin}{\Tunit}}

\inferrule*[right=\rulename{For-Cons}]
  { }
  {\MConfig{\MEnv}{\MErr}{\MPol}{\MPrin}{\Forr{x}{v::vs}{e}} \step \MConfig{\MEnv[\MBind{x}{v}{\tau}{\texttt{let}}]}{\MErr}{\MPol}{\MPrin}{\Seq{e}{\Forr{x}{vs}{e}}}}

\inferrule*[right=\rulename{Seq}]
  { }
  {\MConfig{\MEnv}{\MErr}{\MPol}{\MPrin}{\Seq{v}{e}} \step \MConfig{\MEnv}{\MErr}{\MPol}{\MPrin}{e}}

\inferrule*[right=\rulename{Js}]
  {\JOracle{\MEnv}{c} = v}
  {\MConfig{\MEnv}{\MErr}{\MPol}{\MPrin}{\Js{c}} \step \MConfig{\MEnv}{\MErr}{\MPol}{\MPrin}{v}}
\end{mathpar}

\subsection{Ask, Say, Agent, Eval (L1/L2)}
\label{app:rules-io}

\begin{mathpar}
\inferrule*[right=\rulename{Ask}]
  {\text{human provides }v : \Tstring}
  {\MConfig{\MEnv}{\MErr}{\MPol}{\MPrin}{\Ask{s}} \step \MConfig{\MEnv}{\MErr}{\MPol}{\MPrin}{v}}

\inferrule*[right=\rulename{Say}]
  {\text{emit }s\text{ to output stream}}
  {\MConfig{\MEnv}{\MErr}{\MPol}{\MPrin}{\Say{s}} \step \MConfig{\MEnv}{\MErr}{\MPol}{\MPrin}{\Tunit}}

\inferrule*[right=\rulename{Agent-Success}]
  {\PolOk{\MPol}{\MPrin}{\texttt{agent}} \\
   \Oracle{s}{\MErr}{\Tstring} \ni v \\
   \RtType{\MEnv}{v}{\Tstring}}
  {\MConfig{\MEnv}{\MErr}{\MPol}{\MPrin}{\Agent{s}} \step \MConfig{\MEnv}{\MErr \cdot \SucEv}{\MPol}{\MPrin}{v}}

\inferrule*[right=\rulename{Eval-Success}]
  {\PolOk{\MPol}{\MPrin}{\texttt{eval}} \\
   v = \MEnv(f) \\
   v = \texttt{workflow}\;f(\overline{x_i{:}T_i}) \to T\;\{e_b\} \\
   \forall i.\,\RtType{\MEnv}{v_i}{T_i}}
  {\MConfig{\MEnv}{\MErr}{\MPol}{\MPrin}{\Eval{f}{\overline{v_i}}} \step \MConfig{\MEnv[\overline{\MBind{x_i}{v_i}{T_i}{\texttt{let}}}]}{\MErr}{\MPol}{\MPrin}{e_b}}
\end{mathpar}

The $\rulename{Agent-Success}$ rule mirrors $\rulename{Gen-Success}$ but always returns $\Tstring$, so no separate residual re-type-check is needed: once the oracle value is produced, the head is replaced by $v$ directly, and any surrounding binder (e.g.~$\Let{x}{\Tstring}{\Agent{s}}{e'}$) binds $v$ via a subsequent $\rulename{Let-Bind}$ step lifted by $\rulename{Let-Cong}$. $\rulename{Eval-Success}$ is a root rule that invokes a workflow value that was either defined statically or produced by a prior arrow-typed $\Gen{\Tarrow{A}{R}}{s}$; it lifts to nested positions via the structural congruence rules of~\S\ref{sec:sem-reduction}.

\subsection{Agent and Eval Heal}
\label{app:rules-heal}

Heal rules for $\Agent{s}$ and $\Enforce{x}$ mirror $\rulename{Gen-Heal-Type}$ and $\rulename{Gen-Heal-Pol}$:

\begin{mathpar}
\inferrule*[right=\rulename{Agent-Heal-Type}]
  {\Oracle{s}{\MErr}{\Tstring} \ni v \\
   \lnot\,\RtType{\MEnv}{v}{\Tstring} \\
   \epsilon = \Explain{v}{\Tstring} \\
   \retries{\MErr \cdot \ErrEv{\epsilon}} \le \MRetry}
  {\MConfig{\MEnv}{\MErr}{\MPol}{\MPrin}{\Agent{s}} \step \MConfig{\MEnv}{\MErr \cdot \ErrEv{\epsilon}}{\MPol}{\MPrin}{\Agent{s}}}

\inferrule*[right=\rulename{Enforce-Heal}]
  {\MEnv(x) = (p,\Tpolicy,\_) \\
   \Install{\MPol}{p} \text{ undefined or widens} \\
   \epsilon = \Explain{p}{\MPol} \\
   \retries{\MErr \cdot \ErrEv{\epsilon}} \le \MRetry}
  {\MConfig{\MEnv}{\MErr}{\MPol}{\MPrin}{\Enforce{x}} \step \MConfig{\MEnv}{\MErr \cdot \ErrEv{\epsilon}}{\MPol}{\MPrin}{\Gen{\Tpolicy}{s_x}}}
\end{mathpar}

In $\rulename{Enforce-Heal}$, $s_x$ denotes the prompt supplied by the implementation for the binding of $x$ (retrievable from the defining $\Gen{\Tpolicy}{\cdot}$ site; abstracted as an opaque function in the mechanization). The rule rewinds to a fresh gen of $\Tpolicy$ so the oracle can retry with the Cedar compilation or monotonicity error as feedback; at non-toplevel positions, the structural congruence rules lift it to the surrounding context.

\section{Proof of Theorem~\ref{thm:hadl-sound}}
\label{app:proofs}

We expand the argument clause by clause. Each induction is on the derivation of $C_n \step C_{n+1}$, mirroring the Lean case split on the constructor of $\mathtt{Step}$. The mechanization is free of user-introduced axioms: \texttt{policyInstall\_shrinks} is proved by iterated application of Cedar-Lean's \texttt{unchanged\_deny\_when\_add\_forbid} theorem, and the JavaScript oracle is modelled as a stubbed evaluator \texttt{jsEval} so that \texttt{jsEval\_wellTyped} holds vacuously (host-level evaluation is delegated to the implementation). All theorems depend only on Lean's standard meta-axioms (\texttt{propext}, \texttt{Classical.choice}, \texttt{Quot.sound}); all other steps are sorry-free.

\subsection{(T1) WF-Preservation}

\paragraph{Case $\rulename{Gen-Success}$.} Assume $\WF{C_n}$. We must show $\WF{C_{n+1}}$. Three side conditions of the rule discharge the three clauses of well-formedness:
\begin{itemize}
  \item $\RtType{\MEnv_n}{v}{\tau}$ establishes clause (1) for the head value that replaces $\Gen{\tau}{s}$ in $e_{n+1}$ (the gen rule no longer extends $\MEnv$; a surrounding $\rulename{Let-Cong}/\rulename{Let-Bind}$ handles the extension when the gen sits under a binder).
  \item The residual $e_{n+1}$ type-checks because $v$ substitutes for a $\tau$-typed hole, which is exactly clause (2).
  \item $\MErr_{n+1} = \MErr_n \cdot \SucEv$ has $\retries{\MErr_{n+1}} = 0$ (trailing-success lemma \texttt{ErrCtx.retries\_append\_success}), trivially within budget, establishing clause (3).
\end{itemize}

\paragraph{Case $\rulename{Gen-Heal-Type}$, $\rulename{Gen-Heal-Pol}$, $\rulename{Agent-Heal-*}$, $\rulename{Eval-Heal-*}$, $\rulename{Enforce-Heal}$.} $\MEnv$ is unchanged, so clauses (1)--(2) carry over; clause (3) follows from the rule's own $\retries{\MErr \cdot \ErrEv{\epsilon}} \le \MRetry$ side condition.

\paragraph{Case $\rulename{Gen-Budget-Exhausted}$.} The target configuration has expression $\mathsf{errTerm}(\MErr, \Gen{\tau}{s})$ and is a terminal-error state; by the statement of (T1) we need not prove $\WF{\cdot}$ for terminal errors.

\paragraph{Case $\rulename{Let-Bind}$ (and $\rulename{Var-Bind}$).} The new binding $\MBind{x}{v}{\tau}{\_}$ is well-typed by hypothesis on the evaluation of the right-hand side; the residual expression type-checks because the surrounding let was well-typed at $\tau$. $\MErr$ is preserved unchanged, so the retry budget is trivially maintained.

\paragraph{Case $\rulename{Assign}$.} By the rule, $\MEnv(x) = (\_,\tau,\texttt{var})$ and the assignment writes value $v$ at the same type $\tau$. Clauses (1)--(2) are preserved by the targeted-update lemma (Lean: \texttt{Env.assign\_type\_preserved}); $\MErr$ is unchanged.

\paragraph{Pure-core cases ($\rulename{Var}$, $\rulename{If-*}$, $\rulename{While}$, $\rulename{For-*}$, $\rulename{Seq}$, $\rulename{Js}$).} The environment is unchanged or extended by an iteration binding at the inferred type; $\MPol$ and $\MPrin$ are unchanged. Clauses (1), (3) are trivial. Clause (2) is preserved by the residual typechecker (black-boxed in the mechanization and re-invoked at each $\rulename{Gen-Success}$).

\paragraph{Case $\rulename{Enforce-Install}$.} $\MEnv$ is unchanged; $\MPol$ is updated to $\MPol'$ with $\Allow{\MPol'}\subseteq\Allow{\MPol}$. No binding changes type; $\MErr$ is unchanged.

\paragraph{Cases $\rulename{Agent-Success}$, $\rulename{Eval-Success}$.} Analogous to $\rulename{Gen-Success}$: each carries a runtime-type check as a side condition; the head is replaced by the oracle/workflow value, with any binder updated by a subsequent $\rulename{Let-Bind}$ step. $\MErr$ gains a $\SucEv$ event which resets $\retries{\cdot}$ to $0$.

\paragraph{Cases $\rulename{Let-Cong}$, $\rulename{Assign-Cong}$, $\rulename{If-Cong}$, $\rulename{Seq-Cong}$, $\rulename{For-Cong}$.} By induction hypothesis on the inner step: the inner configuration is well-formed iff the outer is, since the environment, heal context, policy, and principal agree between outer and inner. The $\neq \mathsf{err}$ side condition on the rule rules out propagation of a terminal error back into an evaluating constructor.

\subsection{(T2) Staged Materialization Soundness}

The runtime-type side condition $\RtType{\MEnv_n}{v}{\tau}$ on $\rulename{Gen-Success}$ guarantees that the value substituted for the gen head has the declared type. When the gen sits under $\Let{x}{\tau}{\Gen{\tau}{s}}{e'}$, the subsequent $\rulename{Let-Bind}$ step (lifted by $\rulename{Let-Cong}$ in the usual CBV order) extends $\MEnv$ with $\MBind{x}{v}{\tau}{\texttt{let}}$, so any downstream $\Gen{\tau'}{s'}$ whose annotation refers to $x$ (e.g.~$\Let{y}{x}{\Gen{x}{s'}}{e''}$) is checked against the materialized concrete type $\tau$ rather than against $\Tschema$.

\subsection{(T3) Policy Monotonicity}

$\MPol$ is updated only by $\rulename{Enforce-Install}$, whose side condition enforces $\Allow{\MPol'} \subseteq \Allow{\MPol}$; this is where the mechanization invokes \texttt{policyInstall\_shrinks}. All other rules leave $\MPol$ unchanged. Monotonicity across the whole trace follows by transitivity. The pointwise authorization claim is immediate because each of $\rulename{Gen-Success}$, $\rulename{Agent-Success}$, and $\rulename{Eval-Success}$ has $\PolOk{\MPol}{\MPrin}{a}$ as a side condition.

\subsection{(T4a) Budget Progress}

If at $C_n$ the redex is $\Gen{\tau}{s}$ and $\retries{\MErr_n}>\MRetry$, no success or heal rule is applicable: the success rules require $\PolOk{\cdot}{\cdot}{\texttt{gen}}\wedge\RtType{\cdot}{\cdot}{\cdot}$ to hold for \emph{some} oracle response, which need not exist; the heal rules have $\retries{\MErr\cdot\ErrEv{\epsilon}}\le\MRetry$ as a side condition, which fails. Only $\rulename{Gen-Budget-Exhausted}$ applies, and it deterministically transitions to the terminal-error configuration carrying $\MErr_n$ and the source-span projection $\Prov(\Gen{\tau}{s})$.

\subsection{(T4b) Truthful Gen-Success}

Fix a gen-stuck configuration $C_n$ with $\PolOk{\MPol_n}{\MPrin_n}{\texttt{gen}}$. By eventually-truthfulness (\defn{def:oracle-truthful}) instantiated at $(s,\MEnv_n,\tau,\MPol_n,\MPrin_n)$, there exists $\MErr'$ with $\retries{\MErr'}\le\MRetry$ and $v$ with $\Oracle{s}{\MErr'}{\tau}\ni v$ and $\RtType{\MEnv_n}{v}{\tau}$. The residual static-typing premise is discharged by the $\Tschema$/value wildcards of $\StType{\cdot}{\cdot}{\cdot}$. All side conditions of $\rulename{Gen-Success}$ are met, so the rule fires and appends a $\SucEv$ event to $\MErr$, resetting the retry metric to $0$.

\subsection{(T4c) Oracle-Relative Termination}

Argue by a well-founded measure on reduction sequences. Trace-level gen progress is mechanized by structural induction on the expression under the predicate $\mathsf{HasLeftGen}$: at the leaf $\Gen{\tau}{s}$ head (T4b) fires at the root; at each compound construct (let, assign, if, seq, for), the corresponding structural congruence rule lifts the inner step to the surrounding context, using the $\neq\mathsf{err}$ side condition to discharge the hypothesis that inner reduction did not stall on a terminal error. The agent-case is symmetric with $\mathsf{HasLeftAgent}$. The remaining ingredient --- a well-founded measure on intermediate pure-core reductions --- is argued on paper. Between two successive applications of a generative rule, only pure-core rules fire. Pure-core reduction terminates on any expression whose \texttt{while} and \texttt{for} loops terminate, which we assume of $e_0$. At each generative step, either a success rule fires or a heal rule extends the trailing $\ErrEv{\cdot}$ run of $\MErr$ by one element. Since $\retries{\MErr} \le \MRetry$, at most $\MRetry$ heal steps occur before either (T4b) fires or $\retries{\MErr}>\MRetry$ forces (T4a). Under eventually-truthful $\mathcal{O}$, (T4b) fires within the budget, $\SucEv$ is appended (resetting $\retries{\cdot}$ to $0$), and the process repeats for the next generative site. Since the number of generative sites reached along the trace is finite (bounded by the size of $e_0$ plus finitely many eval expansions), the whole trace terminates. When $\mathcal{O}$ is not eventually-truthful at some site, (T4a) fires and produces a terminal error configuration whose source-span tag, under the well-formedness assumption of Cor.~\ref{cor:blame}, is exactly the failing gen.
