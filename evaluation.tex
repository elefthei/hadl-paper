\section{Evaluation}
\label{sec:evaluation}

We designed the following evaluation to answer four research questions raised by our claims about gradual validation. Numerical results are forthcoming; this section documents the experimental protocol, workflow corpus, metrics, and baseline-comparison methodology.

\subsection{Research Questions and Corpus}
\label{sec:eval-rqs}

\textbf{RQ1 (validation rate):} How often do LLM-generated schemas, workflows, and policies pass \hadl's validator on first attempt?
\textbf{RQ2 (self-healing):} When first-attempt validation fails, does bounded feedback-driven retry converge within $\MRetry$, and at what depth?
\textbf{RQ3 (overhead):} What is the latency and token-cost overhead of gradual validation and per-invocation Cedar authorization relative to an untyped baseline?
\textbf{RQ4 (baselines):} How does \hadl compare quantitatively and qualitatively to LangGraph~\cite{langchain2022}, LMQL~\cite{beurer2023prompting}, and DSPy~\cite{khattab2023dspy}?

The corpus contains six \hadl workflows --- the two illustrative ones from \sect{sec:intro} and the overview plus four new tasks chosen to exercise the three gradual-validation modes and per-invocation authorization (customer-support triage, code-review agent, financial-compliance report, research-paper summarizer). Each (workflow, model) pair is executed $K$ times against three frontier LLMs (GPT-4.1, Claude-Sonnet-4, and the default Copilot SDK model), with $\MRetry = 3$ and a five-category error taxonomy (success, type rejection, policy rejection, policy-install failure, runtime error). \sect{app:eval-method} details the methodology, corpus, and determinism controls.

The closest design (DSPy's signatures) operates at module level, not workflow level; LangGraph and LMQL have no built-in typed retry.

\subsection{Threats to Validity}
\label{sec:eval-threats}

Three LLMs is a narrow sample, and smaller models would exercise retry more heavily; six workflows exercise all three gradual-validation modes but do not yet constitute a representative benchmark; the evaluation measures cooperative-oracle behaviour and should not be read as evidence for the adversarial threats of \sect{sec:design-threat}. A dedicated adversarial study is future work.
